🧾 TypeScript – Strongly Bound Magic

When you want your spells to be safe, consistent, and warded against chaos — you use TypeScript.

✨ What is TypeScript?

TypeScript is a mystical extension of JavaScript that adds types. These are spellbound contracts — clear expectations for what your variables, functions, and objects should be.

With TypeScript, your spells are harder to break. Errors are revealed before you ever cast the code.

🔐 Declaring Types

When you declare a variable, you can lock in what it must hold — like labeling a potion bottle “Fire Only.”

let mana: number = 100;
let spellName: string = "Fireball";

📦 Interfaces – Contracted Spell Structure

Interfaces define the expected shape of an object — like a scroll template every caster must follow.

interface Spell {
  name: string;
  cost: number;
}

let fireball: Spell = {
  name: "Fireball",
  cost: 30
};

🧙 Why Use TypeScript?

  • ✨ Find mistakes before they explode
  • 🔎 Make your intent crystal clear
  • 📚 Get better auto-suggestions and documentation
  • 🔐 Protect your future self from ambiguity
